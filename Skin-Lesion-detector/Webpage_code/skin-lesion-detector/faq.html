Purpose

This web app uses computer vision to identify the three highest probability diagnoses for a skin lesion.

Lesion Types

Melanocytic nevus

Melanocytic nevus is the medical term for a mole. They are benign(non-cancerous) and typically do not require treatment. Rarely, melanocytic nevi can become cancerous.

Melanoma

The most serious type of skin cancer. Most melanomas are caused by exposure to ultraviolet light.

Actinic keratosis

Actinic keratosis is a precancerous scaly spot found on sun-damaged skin.

Benign keratosis

Benign keratosis, also known as seborrheic keratosis, is a non-cancerous skin tumor. These growths are slow-growing, raised, and can have a rough texture or look scaly. They are more likely to appear as people age.

Basal Cell Carcinoma

Basal cell carcinoma is a common variant of epithelial skin cancer that rarely metastasizes but grows destructively if untreated. It appears in different morphologic variants (flat, nodular, pigmented, cystic, etc.).

Vascular skin lesions

Vascular skin lesions range from angiomas to angiokeratomas and pyogenic granulomas. Hemorrhage is also included in this category.

Dermatofibroma

Dermatofibroma is a benign skin lesion regarded as either a benign proliferation or an inflammatory reaction to minimal trauma. It is brown often showing a central zone of fibrosis dermatoscopically.

Image format

The app accepts images in jpg or png format.

Limitations

The Deep Learning model that powers this app is not skilled enough to consistently assign the highest score to the correct lesion. Although, the model is trained good enough to detect melanoma better than others, its still better to consult a dermatologist for final opinion. Also, during testing, the correct lesion was regularly among the top 3 predicted lesions.

What do the percentage numbers mean?

They show how confident the model is that a particular condition is present on the image. For example, Melanoma(mel)-23.50% means that the model is 23.5% sure that the lesion shown in the image is melanoma.

How do I use this app on a computer?

- Go to the site on your computer.
- Click on "Load Model" button.
- Click on "Demo Image" and "Predict" button for checking prediction on demo image.
- Click on "Upload image" and click "Open" to upload your lesion photo; then click on "Predict" button.
- The app will print results for the image along with percentages of accuracy for top 3 predictions.

How do I use this app on a mobile phone?

You can take photos of lesion and submit photos that are stored on your phone the same way as described above for computer.
